The Autobiography of Kline C. Avila

Kline C. Avila

My name is Kline C. Avila, and I was born on July 4, 2001. My parents are Roger W. Avila and Helen C. Avila. I have four siblings, making us a total of five, which consists of three girls and two boys.

In my free time, I enjoy simple yet meaningful hobbies. I love watching movies as they allow me to explore different stories and perspectives from around the world. I also enjoy reading books, which help me expand my imagination and knowledge while offering peace and relaxation. Aside from my interest in films and literature, I am also a dog and fish lover. I have a Shih Tzu named Mocho, who brings joy and comfort to my everyday life, and a betta fish that adds color and calmness to my home. Taking care of them teaches me patience, love, responsibility, and compassion.

I was born and raised in Lapu-Lapu City, and after completing my primary education in Bankal, we relocated to Compostela, Cebu, where my father resided before meeting my mother, and there, I pursued my secondary education. Eventually, after finishing my junior high school education, I returned to Lapu-Lapu City to pursue my desired track, which is Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS), as my former school did not offer this strand.

Upon graduating, my reality did not unfold as I had envisioned. During the lockdown phase of the COVID-19 pandemic, I found myself in the middle of an online college admission interview when my connection dropped, resulting in the interview being interrupted due to the poor signal in my area. I was given another opportunity to resume the interview, but unfortunately, it still did not succeed.

And then I made a significant decision to leave school and pursue employment. After spending four years in the workforce, I came to a realization that time moves quickly, and I am not getting any younger. Those years of working gave me valuable experiences, lessons, friendship, met the person who loved me for who I am, and a deeper understanding of responsibility and perseverance. However, I also felt that something was missing — my dream of completing my studies and building a better future for myself.

With that realization, I made one of the most important decisions in my life: to stop working and continue pursuing my education. It wasn’t an easy choice, but I knew that investing in knowledge is something that will last a lifetime. I want to improve myself, not just for my own success but also to make a meaningful contribution to others in the future. Returning to school is both a challenge and a new beginning for me. It’s a step toward personal growth, a reminder that it’s never too late to chase your dreams, and that learning has no age limit.
